# E. Risk and Review List

## Claims that need human legal review

- Contract length cap. The consolidated Federal Decree-Law 33 of 2021 text (updated 29 July 2024) has no maximum contract length in Article 8(3), yet the u.ae "Job offers and the employment process" page still says "not exceeding three years". The pages follow the law text. A reviewer should confirm the amendment history (Federal Decree-Law 14 of 2022 is commonly cited as removing the cap; the amendment was not opened directly).
- NOC and job changes. MOHRE's published transfer permit conditions do not list an NOC, and the page says exactly that. Do not strengthen this to "the NOC is abolished" without a direct official statement. The u.ae "Work permit for a new job" page returned 404.
- The 15-day wage delay rule. Verified on an official MOHRE news page (June 2022), not in the text of Cabinet Resolution No. 1 of 2022 (the HTML page returned 403 and the PDF extraction did not surface the 15-day wording). Needs a manual check if a statutory citation is wanted.
- Free zone scope. Decree-Law 33/2021 Article 3 does not expressly exclude ordinary free zones, yet u.ae says each zone sets its own employment rules. The pages follow u.ae's framing. Known grey area outside DIFC and ADGM.
- Tips and service charge distribution. No official source found on staff distribution rules. The pay page treats distribution strictly as employer policy and the Dubai "10% under resolution" claim from the old glossary was softened to "commonly 10 per cent" because the Dubai instrument could not be opened. If the site wants the stronger Dubai claim back, it needs the resolution itself.

- The AED 50,000 complaint threshold. u.ae says MOHRE decides claims "less than AED 50,000"; the amending law (Decree-Law 20 of 2023) is often summarised as "not exceeding AED 50,000". The page follows u.ae's wording. Boundary cases are ambiguous; check if precision matters.
- "A false absconding report is a serious employer offence" is an inference from the cancellation grounds in Ministerial Resolution 47/2022 and general MOHRE enforcement; no specific penalty figure was verified. Soften or cut if zero inference is wanted.
- The u.ae labour dispute page attributes the one-year claim window to "Article 10", which looks like a page-side inconsistency. The one-year figure is confirmed; the article number was left out of the page text.

- Sick leave notification: u.ae says "within maximum three days"; some translations of Article 31 say "three working days". The page uses the stricter "within three days". Worth a glance at Article 31.
- Leave during probation: the annual-leave timing is derived from the 6-month accrual rule in Article 29, not an explicit "no annual leave in probation" clause. Page wording is hedged accordingly.
- The 56-hour shift-averaging figure and the 144-hours-per-3-weeks overtime cap were verified via fetched summaries of the law and regulation texts, matched across two sources, but the exact clause wording was not read verbatim.
- The Ramadan reduction "applies to all workers" rests on the federal texts being silent on religion (contrast ADGM, which limits its cut to Muslim employees). Standard reading, flagged for transparency.

- The AED 9,000 per month 2026 Emiratisation contribution is derived arithmetic (AED 6,000 in 2023 plus AED 1,000 per year, per the official u.ae schedule). No official page stating "AED 9,000" verbatim could be opened. Confirm against MOHRE's 2026 announcement before publishing.
- Free zones and Emiratisation is the weakest claim in the package. No official page explicitly confirming inclusion or exclusion could be opened. The page keeps the wording conditional ("check with your zone authority; boundary under review"). A human check of the MOHRE FAQ is advised before publication.
- Work injury reporting deadline: the dedicated u.ae work injury page says 48 hours; another u.ae summary says 24 hours for injuries and deaths. The page uses 48 hours. Check Ministerial Resolution 657 of 2022 manually.
- The 2026 WPS resolution (Ministerial Resolution 340 of 2026) is new. The escalation steps carry high change risk and only the two most load-bearing were used (day-1 due date, day-5 permit block).
- Nafis headline target: u.ae's Emirati employment page says 75,000 over five years; the Projects of the 50 page summary said 170,000. The page uses 75,000 from the more recently updated source and omits per-scheme AED amounts because nafis.gov.ae would not open.
- No official source states what happens to the 20 to 49 employee group in 2026. The page says no further step had been announced. Re-check before publication.
- The 1% H1 2026 target deadline was confirmed partly via MOHRE's official X account because the MOHRE news page body would not load.

## Rules likely to change soon

- Basic health insurance package price (AED 320/year) and the nationwide rollout of permit-linked insurance: high change risk.
- Work permit types, counts and fee bands: medium change risk; MOHRE revises these often.
- MOHRE unpaid-wage escalation timeline: medium change risk; ministerial resolutions in this area change fairly often.
- Emiratisation targets and contribution amounts: see the emiratisation-compliance rows once verified; always keep "as of" dating.
- ILOE band boundary wording (AED 16,000 "or less" vs "below"): confirm on iloe.ae if precision matters.

## Possible duplication between pages

- Overtime appears on both pay-benefits (rates) and leave-working-hours (hour limits). The split is deliberate; each page carries one sentence pointing to the other. Watch this boundary in future edits.
- WPS appears on pay-benefits (worker-facing) and emiratisation-compliance (employer penalties). Same deliberate split.
- Probation appears fully on employment-contracts; termination-gratuity and leave-working-hours reference it in one sentence only.
- Basic salary is defined on pay-benefits; termination-gratuity references it only as the gratuity base.
