The GCC Recruitment Guide: Working and Hiring Across the Gulf

The Gulf is one of the most active job markets on earth. Every year, thousands of people move to the UAE, Saudi Arabia, Qatar, and the wider region to build careers that would take far longer to build at home. This guide is being built to help you do exactly that, the smart way.

I am an HR Career Specialist, and I have spent my career inside Gulf hiring. I have read the CVs, sat on the panels, and made the calls. This guide turns that inside view into plain, practical help for both job seekers and the people who hire them.

What this guide covers

The GCC recruitment guide is growing into a full map of how hiring works across the Gulf. It will cover how employers screen and choose people, how candidates can stand out, and how the rules of work differ from one country to the next. The aim is simple. Cut the guesswork, and replace it with clear steps that actually work in this region.

The Gulf does not hire the way the West does. The CV norms differ, the interview style differs, and the visa rules shape everything. A guide written for London or New York will quietly mislead you here. This one is built for the Gulf, by someone who works in it.

Start here: internships

The first full section of this guide is about internships. For students and early-career people, an internship is often the cleanest way into the Gulf job market. It is a chance to prove yourself, learn the local way of working, and turn a short placement into a real job.

If you are looking to start, go straight to the internships in Dubai and the UAE guide. It covers how to find and apply for an internship, how to build an intern CV, what employers really want, and how to turn an internship into a full-time offer. The hospitality sector, one of the Gulf's biggest employers of interns, has its own dedicated page too.

More on the way

This pillar will expand over time into a complete recruitment resource for the Gulf. That means deeper guidance on hiring across the UAE, Saudi Arabia, and Qatar, on writing job descriptions and screening CVs, on interview stages, work visas, and the nationalisation rules that shape who gets hired and how.

For now, the internships section is the place to begin. Whether you are a student chasing your first placement or an employer building a pipeline of young talent, the guidance there is ready to use today.

The Gulf rewards people who understand how it really works. Keep this guide close as it grows, and you will always have a clear, honest view of how careers are built and how hiring is done across the region. Begin with the internships guide, and check back as new sections go live.

This guide gives general information, not legal or recruitment advice. Rules and practices change across the Gulf, so confirm specifics with the relevant authority before you act.
