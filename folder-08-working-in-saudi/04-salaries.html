Saudi Arabia Salaries 2026: What Expatriates Actually Earn

What does Saudi Arabia actually pay expatriates in 2026? The honest answer involves three numbers, not one: the headline salary, the value of the housing and benefits stacked on top, and what the same offer would deliver after you account for the cost of the life around it. This page lays out real bands for the roles people ask me about most, then teaches you to read them properly.

I am an HR Career Specialist, and these ranges come from years of seeing Saudi offers up close. Treat every band as a starting point, not a promise. Saudi packages vary more by employer and by giga-project status than UAE ones do, so use these as the first reading and triangulate with your sector recruiter.

Engineering and project management

Engineering pays well in Saudi Arabia, especially on the giga-projects. A mid-level civil or mechanical engineer often earns between SAR 18,000 and SAR 35,000 a month. Senior engineers and project managers commonly sit between SAR 35,000 and SAR 65,000. Engineering directors and project principals on major giga-projects can earn SAR 70,000 to SAR 130,000 or more, with substantial bonus and benefits on top.

The premium attached to NEOM, the Red Sea Project, and similar developments is real. I have placed engineers and project managers into these projects whose packages ran ten to twenty percent above equivalent roles at conventional employers, partly to compensate for remote-site working and partly because demand outstrips supply. So always ask about giga-project allowances on top of the base when you negotiate.

Hospitality and tourism

Hospitality is a fast-growing Saudi sector, driven by Vision 2030 tourism targets. A front office manager often earns SAR 14,000 to SAR 22,000 a month. A food and beverage manager sits between SAR 18,000 and SAR 32,000. An executive chef at a strong five-star property can earn SAR 40,000 to SAR 70,000. General managers of major hotels commonly earn SAR 50,000 to SAR 90,000, with the very top properties paying above that.

I have seen many hospitality offers in Saudi Arabia include provided accommodation, transport, and meals on duty, which removes large costs from your life. A junior or mid-level role can deliver a much higher real saving rate than the headline suggests once you price the provided benefits properly. Always ask about the full package, not just the monthly figure.

Healthcare

Healthcare salaries in Saudi Arabia range widely with specialism, licence, and hospital tier. A registered nurse usually earns between SAR 8,000 and SAR 18,000 a month, with specialist licences and experience pushing higher. A dentist in general practice often sits between SAR 20,000 and SAR 40,000, with specialists above that. Consultants and senior specialists at major hospital groups can earn well into six figures monthly.

Saudi healthcare offers often include provided housing for senior roles, family medical insurance, and substantial relocation packages. So compare not just the cash, but the total package against the same role in another Gulf country, because the real net saving rate is often what differs most.

Finance, banking, and professional services

Finance and banking sit broadly close to UAE equivalents at senior level, with stronger bonus and incentive structures in some firms. A mid-level finance manager often earns SAR 25,000 to SAR 45,000 a month. A senior financial controller commonly earns SAR 40,000 to SAR 70,000. Senior commercial banking and asset management roles can earn well above that, especially in Riyadh.

The headline is not the offer.

Technology and digital roles

Technology is one of the fastest-moving salary markets in Saudi Arabia, driven by sovereign digital projects, fintech expansion, and the giga-projects' technology spend. A mid-level software engineer often earns SAR 20,000 to SAR 38,000 a month. Senior engineers, tech leads, and architects commonly earn SAR 38,000 to SAR 70,000. Scarce skills in AI, cybersecurity, and cloud architecture sit higher, with the best landing six-figure monthly packages.

Like the UAE, I have noticed Saudi tech roles increasingly pay in equity at venture-backed and large global employers, which can lift the real annual value 20 to 40 percent above the headline. Ask about long-term incentive plans and stock grants explicitly. The headline alone often understates the real annual compensation by a meaningful margin.

HR, marketing, and shared services

HR and marketing functions sit in the middle of the Saudi pay tables. An HR business partner often earns SAR 22,000 to SAR 38,000 a month. A senior marketing manager often sits between SAR 28,000 and SAR 55,000. An HR director at a major group can earn SAR 60,000 to SAR 110,000 or more. Shared services functions follow similar patterns, with sector specialism lifting the number.

I once helped an HR director move from Dubai to Riyadh on a package that looked similar in headline cash, but included accommodation worth around SAR 240,000 a year that her UAE role had not. So always price the provided housing carefully, because in Saudi Arabia it is often the single biggest hidden component of the package.

The benefits that quietly change the maths

Three Saudi benefits matter more than candidates expect. Provided or allowance-based housing is often substantial, especially in Riyadh, where compound rents are high. Provided or covered schooling for two or more children can be worth tens of thousands of riyals a year. And generous annual leave, often six weeks for senior roles, with paid flights home for the family, adds real cash value once you cost the trips.

None of these is automatic. They are negotiated, and good employers state them clearly in the offer letter. So always ask for the full benefits picture in writing, item by item. Common questions about Saudi Arabia salaries

Are salaries higher in Saudi Arabia or the UAE?
It depends on the role, the sector, and the package structure. Many Saudi senior roles, especially on giga-projects, pay at or above UAE equivalents, with provided housing and schooling adding real value. Compare net packages, not headline cash, to judge fairly.

Is there income tax on Saudi salaries?
No personal income tax on salary. What is offered is what reaches your account, which keeps Saudi pay genuinely close to its headline number, and lifts savings rates significantly compared to high-tax markets.

Do Saudi salary packages include housing?
Often yes, especially for senior expatriate roles. Provided housing in a compound, a substantial housing allowance, or both, are common parts of the package. Always ask about the housing component up front, because it can change the real value of the offer dramatically.
