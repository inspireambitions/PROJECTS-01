## The core problem, restated simply

Right now the sequence reads as written for people who already have Gulf
hospitality jobs, dropped into a stream that a lot of not-yet-hired readers
also join. Rights and pay content (gratuity, end-of-service) is only
useful once someone is employed. Placed early, it signals "this newsletter
isn't for me yet" to exactly the audience segment (job seekers abroad) the
subscribe page works hardest to attract.

## Recommended fix — two options

**Option A (preferred): light reorder, no full rewrite.**
Move any not-yet-employed content (job search, CVs, scams, interviews) to
emails 1–3. Push rights/pay/gratuity content to emails 5–6, after trust is
established and after the reader has had at least one email that assumes
they're hired or about to be. Email 7 stays as the universal action close.

Proposed reordered shape:
1. "are you building a career, or just surviving?" — opener, both audiences (unchanged)
2. "don't pay someone to access a job." — job seeker, scam warning (unchanged — already well placed)
3. "why your CV gets ignored" — job seeker, CV fix (moved up from #4)
4. "what managers really want to hear" — reframe explicitly as *interview* signal for job seekers AND *day-to-day* signal for employed readers, i.e. address both readers by name in one email ("If you're interviewing... If you're already on the floor...")
5. "hard work won't get you promoted" — already-in-Gulf, promotion mechanics (moved down from #3)
6. "never say your number first" — reframe to cover both interview salary negotiation and performance-review negotiation, or split the guidance visibly by audience within the email
7. "what to do Monday, not your whole career" — universal close (unchanged)

The rights/pay/gratuity material referenced in Kim's brief should sit in
whichever numbered slot it currently occupies, moved to position 5 or 6
under this order, with a one-line audience note added near the top (see
Option B below) regardless of position — cheap insurance against the same
complaint recurring.

**Option B (minimum viable fix, ship this week): one-line addition, no
reorder.**
Keep the current order. Add a single sentence near the top of the
rights/pay email that tells a job seeker this content is for later and
points them to the get-hired email:

> "This one's for after you've landed the role. If you're still job
> hunting, start here instead: [link to the CV or scam-warning email /
> resource]."

This does not fix the sequencing problem, but it directly answers the
question Ewi asked, in every future send, at near-zero editorial cost.

**Recommendation:** ship Option B this week as the immediate fix (it
directly answers a reader question already in hand), then do Option A as
the next full sequence revision once the actual body copy can be pulled
from Resend and reviewed end to end.
