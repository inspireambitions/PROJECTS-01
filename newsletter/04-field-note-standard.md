# Field Note standard

The Field Note is the signature close of every issue. It is the one part
readers are most likely to remember and act on, per the subscribe page's
own pitch: "One small action to take. A line to send, a question to ask, a
number to check, a document to fix."

## The standard

Every issue's Field Note must give the reader **one concrete action they
can take before their next shift or application** — not a reflection, not
"consider whether...", not a general principle. A checklist to hold every
draft against before it ships:

1. **Names one action**, not a menu of options. If there's a version for
   each audience, give exactly one action per audience, clearly labelled
   (see the sample Memo on the subscribe page for the pattern: "Already in
   the Gulf?" / "Applying from abroad?").
2. **Is doable within 24–48 hours.** Not "over the coming months" — "before
   your next shift," "before you send your next application," "before
   Friday."
3. **Is concrete enough to fail or succeed.** A line to send (give the
   actual wording, or close to it), a specific number to check, a specific
   document to open and fix. Not "review your CV" — "check whether your CV
   lists a phone number with the UAE country code."
4. **Costs nothing and needs no one's permission.** The reader should be
   able to do it alone, without a manager's sign-off or a purchase.
5. **Ties back to the issue's topic**, not a generic sign-off. If the issue
   is about the 60-day hiring window, the Field Note is about acting inside
   that window, not a general "stay proactive" line.
6. **Is under 80 words.** If it needs more, it's not concrete enough yet —
   cut it down to the single action, not the reasoning behind it.

## Quick self-test before sending any issue

Read only the Field Note, out of context. Could a tired reader, on their
phone, on a break, do the thing in it right now without re-reading the rest
of the issue? If not, rewrite it.
