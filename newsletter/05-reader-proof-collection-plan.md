# Reader-proof collection plan

The subscribe page currently has an honest placeholder: "Reader notes will
go here. We will not fake social proof." This plan fills that space
honestly over time, without fabricating anything.

## Format (matches the page's existing example)

Short, anonymised, by role and city only — e.g. "Front Office Agent,
Dubai" or "Aspiring Chef, Nairobi." No full names, no employer names, no
photos, matching the editorial rule against exposing employer or colleague
detail.

## How to collect, consent-first

1. **Source: real reply-backs.** Readers already reply to issues (see the
   Ewi Cletus example). When a reply contains a genuinely positive,
   usable line, that's the only honest source, not solicited testimonials
   written to order.
2. **Always ask before publishing.** Never quote a reply without asking
   first. Draft ask, sent as a normal reply:

   > "Would you be alright if I quoted a short, anonymised version of that
   > on the newsletter's subscribe page? I'd only use your role and city,
   > nothing else, no employer names, no photo, and you can ask me to take
   > it down any time."

3. **Log consent.** Keep a simple record (role, city, the exact quote used,
   date consent given) somewhere Kim controls, not in the newsletter
   platform itself. A private note or spreadsheet is enough. This protects
   Kim if a reader later asks what was published and why.
4. **Trim, don't invent.** Edit only for length and clarity. Never add a
   claim, a number, or a sentiment the reader didn't write.
5. **Threshold before publishing anything.** Don't add the first note
   until there are at least 3–5 consented notes, so the section doesn't
   read as one lonely quote. Until then, the honest placeholder stays as
   is, which is already doing its job.
6. **Refresh periodically.** Rotate in newer notes every few months so the
   section doesn't look static or stale.

## What never goes in

Fabricated numbers ("98% of readers..."), stock stock-photo headshots,
full names, employer names, or any note that wasn't given with explicit
consent to publish.
