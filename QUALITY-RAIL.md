# Inspire Ambitions — Binding Content Quality Rail

**Source:** `IA QUALITY CHECK..xlsx` (51-point Unified Checklist, March 2026). This version supersedes the older 46-point `Unified_Content_Quality_Checklist` and `SAUDI.xlsx`. Use this one only.

**Rule:** Every piece of content must follow this rail at all times. Target 51/51 (dual-publish) or 36/36 (Medium-only). Any single failure = revise before publish.

---

## Scoring
- Checks 1–36 apply to EVERY article (Medium and inspireambitions.com).
- Checks 37–46 apply ONLY when publishing on inspireambitions.com.
- Checks 47–51 (Section 8) are the anti-template rules, applied across the article series.
- Dual-publish: 51/51 publish, 47–50 fix flagged, 43–46 revise, below 43 rewrite.
- Medium-only: 36/36 publish, 33–35 fix, below 33 rewrite.

## Section 1 — Headline, subtitle, structure
1. Headline under 110 chars, 8–12 words, real hook (number/provocation/personal stake). No clickbait, no ALL CAPS, no question mark unless answered.
2. Subtitle 15–25 words, expands the hook, signals expertise without a credentials line.
3. Meta description 120–160 chars, primary keyword natural, not stuffed.
4. Word count **1,200–1,500** (hard reject under 1,200 or over 1,800).
5. Mobile-first paragraphs: 2–4 sentences max, none over 5 mobile lines / 100 words, ~45–50% single-sentence paragraphs.
6. Scannable H2 every 200–300 words; describe content, never tease ("Tips for Success" = reject).
7. No clickbait language (Feb 2026 Discover update). Title delivers exactly what it promises.

## Section 2 — Opening, body, closing
8. Hook in first 3 sentences. State the problem immediately, no preamble, no definition opening.
9. 3–5 body sections, 200–300 words each, each with a subheading and at least one concrete example/data point/operational detail.
10. **Data density: 5+ named, attributed statistics or data points per article.** No "studies show" / "research suggests" without naming the source.
11. Actionable specific advice ("message someone in the department before you apply", not "network more").
12. Strong declarative close: a position statement, not a summary, not a question. Exactly one link to a specific inspireambitions.com page (not homepage).
13. Newsletter CTA at end of every article.
14. Tags: exactly 5 (Medium) — 2 broad, 1 location, 1 niche, all high-traffic.

## Section 3 — E-E-A-T
15. 8–10 first-person markers per article ("I have seen", "In my experience"), natural not forced.
16. At least 2 real personal anecdotes from the career. Composited/anonymised is fine. Not "a friend told me".
17. Credentials (HR Career Specialist, 20+ years) within first 300 words, woven in, NOT a standalone "After 20 years in HR" cliche.
18. Contrarian or original angle. Operator view is the differentiator.
19. Topic authority: Gulf careers, HR operations, Africa-to-Gulf relocation, hospitality.
20. Author byline: full name, HR Career Specialist, date, link to author bio page. Never the real employer title.

## Section 4 — AI detection guardrails
21. ZERO blacklisted AI phrases anywhere: furthermore, moreover, delve, it's important to note, landscape, notably, specifically, essentially, ultimately, consequently, in conclusion, to summarise, crucial, realm, foster, navigate, leverage.
22. Sentence burstiness: ~50% short (<12 words), ~40% medium (12–25), ~10% long (25+). No 3 consecutive same-length sentences.
23. Varied sentence starters: no more than 3 sentences starting with the same word per 10-sentence block.
24. No formulaic transitions: "That said", "Having said that", "It is worth noting", "With that in mind", "At the end of the day", "In today's world".
25. NO em dashes anywhere. Commas, full stops, or restructure.
26. Active voice dominant: passive under 10%.
27. British English throughout: organisation, prioritise, colour, centre, analyse.
28. Human voice test: sounds like a person talking when read aloud.

## Section 5 — Employer safety
29. No company data leaked (no employer name, internal process, confidential metric, identifiable colleague).
30. No colleague identification. All examples composited/anonymised.
31. Employer-safe positioning: thought leader, not whistleblower.
32. Job title: "HR Career Specialist" only. Never the real Director-level title.
33. Reddit-proof: survives r/jobs or r/humanresources without "AI slop" criticism.

## Section 6 — Medium publication fit
34. Publication scope match (read 3 recent articles from the target pub first).
35. Match the publication's formatting conventions.
36. Canonical link set via Medium Import for republished content.

## Section 7 — inspireambitions.com technical (checks 37–46)
37. Featured image 1200px+, original (not stock), 16:9 or 4:3, alt text with primary keyword.
38. `max-image-preview:large` meta tag in head.
39. Article schema markup (author, datePublished, dateModified, publisher, image).
40. SEO title under 60 chars (plugin = AIOSEO on this site, the checklist says "Yoast" generically).
41. SEO meta description 120–160 chars with primary keyword.
42. Focus keyword set, green/orange score minimum.
43. Category assigned (Career Growth or relevant). Never Uncategorised.
44. URL slug clean, keyword-rich, no stop words, matches plan.
45. Internal links: minimum 2 to related articles.
46. External links open in new tab (`target="_blank"`).

## Section 8 — Anti-template (checks 47–51, applied across the series)
47. Structure rotation: never the same article structure twice in a row. Rotate: (1) cold-open narrative, (2) single argument no subheadings, (3) contrarian lead, (4) data-first breakdown, (5) question-driven.
48. Never use "The Bottom Line" as a closing heading.
49. Numbered action steps in at most 1 of every 3 articles, embedded in prose not as standalone lists.
50. Never open two consecutive articles with a statistic.
51. "20+ years in HR" credential at most once per 5 articles. Otherwise signal authority through specific observations and named frameworks.

## Extra usage notes (from the sheet)
- None of the content should sound similar or generic. Must pass AI-detector tools. Must feel authoritative, never vague.
