The five-year question is not about your dreams.

It's about their risk.

When a hotel asks where you see yourself in five years, they're really asking:
"If we train you, will it pay back?"

So answer the risk. A fictional model:

"In five years I see myself as a team leader or supervisor in this department.
To get there I need to master this role first, then build my skills in training others and handling the tougher shifts.
What I like about this hotel is that people can grow without leaving — and that's exactly what I want. To grow here, not away from here."

Three parts:

→ A realistic next role.
→ The skills you know it needs.
→ The message that your growth and their retention are the same plan.

Ambition that stays is the answer every hotel wants to hear.
