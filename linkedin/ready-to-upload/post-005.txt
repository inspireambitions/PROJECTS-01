"Tell me about yourself" is not an invitation to tell your life story.

It's a thirty-second audition.

The formula: present, proof, reason.

A full model answer:

"I'm a guest service professional with three years in busy front-of-house roles.
In my last position I handled check-in for a two-hundred-room property and kept my queue moving during full occupancy.
I'm applying here because I want to grow in a five-star environment that takes service standards seriously."

That's it. Thirty seconds.

Now take the three blanks and make them yours:

→ Your role.
→ One result you can defend with detail.
→ One honest reason this hotel.

Practise it aloud five times before the interview.

Not memorised. Owned.

What role are you interviewing for? Comment and I'll suggest your proof line.
